# What I Got Wrong [​](https://danielmiessler.com/predictions/#what-i-got-wrong)
These are my notable misses, along with what I learned from each. I put this section near the top because learning from errors matters more than celebrating wins.
## The Current President and Russia [​](https://danielmiessler.com/predictions/#the-current-president-and-russia)
**Prediction** : He would officially end the war in Ukraine by April 1, 2025, capitulating completely to Russia.
**Confidence** : Probable (70%)
**What Actually Happened** : A messy middle ground with mixed signals—some Russian opposition, some Ukraine support. Not the clear capitulation I expected.
**What I Learned** : Stop assuming I "get" complex personalities. My bias toward believing I've nailed someone's model leads to overconfident predictions about their behavior. The lesson isn't that he's unpredictable—it's that I should be far more humble about predicting individual behavior.
**Model Update** : When predicting individual behavior, weight structural incentives more heavily than personality models. And dramatically widen confidence intervals.
## Twitter Exodus Timeline [​](https://danielmiessler.com/predictions/#twitter-exodus-timeline)
**Prediction** : People would return to Twitter within 6 months after the mass exodus (December 2022)
**Confidence** : Almost Certain (90%+)
**What Actually Happened** : Some returned, but not to the degree or timeline predicted. The exodus platforms felt like ghost towns, but the return was slower and messier than expected.
**What I Learned** : I overvalued network effects and undervalued the role of trust in platform loyalty. People's willingness to switch platforms is more about leadership alignment than convenience.
**Model Update** : When predicting social platform behavior, weight perceived values alignment between users and leadership more heavily than pure network effects.
## Elon's Political Trajectory [​](https://danielmiessler.com/predictions/#elon-s-political-trajectory)
**Prediction** : None specific, but I generally did not see Elon going far-right in 2023-2024.
**Confidence** : N/A
**What Actually Happened** : Someone I thought was center and humanist became mean, angry, and hateful. Looking back, the signs were there (child, trans issue), but I missed them.
**What I Learned** : Personal grievances can dramatically reshape someone's public persona in ways that aren't predictable from their prior behavior. Emotional wounds matter more than stated values.
## Apple AI Timeline [​](https://danielmiessler.com/predictions/#apple-ai-timeline)
**Prediction** : Apple would go from worst to best AI implementation within months (January 2025)
**Confidence** : Probable (70%)
**What Actually Happened** : A year later, still hasn't happened. Apple Intelligence adoption underwhelming. Siri overhaul keeps getting pushed back.
**What I Learned** : I underestimated Apple's organizational challenges with AI specifically. Their usual pattern of "late but polished" may not apply to rapidly-moving AI capabilities.

# Methodology & Purpose [​](https://danielmiessler.com/predictions/#methodology-purpose)
The goal isn't proving how smart I am. It's showing I make predictions, track them honestly, learn from mistakes, and update my models. That builds more credibility than any "I was right" list.
**Rules:**
  1. Predictions are locked once made—no material changes
  2. I use the CIA's [Words of Estimative Probability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Words_of_estimative_probability): Almost Certain (90%+), Probable (70%), Chances About Even (50%), Probably Not (30%), Almost Certainly Not (10%)
  3. Obvious predictions omitted—they don't seem worth tracking
  4. AI helps identify my biases—feed your list to a model and ask for patterns
