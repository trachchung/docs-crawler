## Cashback
When you have an offer or discount campaign running, you can use Speed's cashback resource to reward your customers. With cashback, you can automatically send funds to your customers as soon as they have made the full payment. Once the transaction is complete, your customer can claim the reward they have earned.
By using cashback, you can effortlessly send funds to your customers once they have made the full payment. Whether you have received the payment on-chain or through the Lightning Network, your customer will receive the funds through Lightning(LNURL). Hence, to claim this cashback, your customer needs an LNURL-supported lightning wallet.
Upon full payment made by a customer, if cashback is applicable, i.e., attached to a Speed’s receive payment functionality (i.e., Checkout link , Payment link, or Checkout session), where the end date of cashback has not passed, a scannable QR code will be generated by the system. The customer can scan this QR code and redeem the cashback amount in their LNURL-supported wallet.
#### [Cashback lifecycleCopied!](https://docs.tryspeed.com/docs/send-payments/cashback#cashback-lifecycle)
When the cashback is created, it will have an active status until it is deactivated. Hence, you can now **[associate this cashback](https://docs.tryspeed.com/docs/manage-cashbacks#associate-a-cashback)** while creating any of the Speed’s receive-payments resources, like payment links, checkout links, and checkout sessions.
When your customer makes a full payment, if applicable, they can avail cashback by scanning the QR code that opens in a web browser. Customers do not need a Speed account to claim cashback. Once they claim the cashback, Speed will process the cashback’s withdrawal associated with that checkout session and mark it as paid. You can see that the withdraw is marked as paid in the listings available on the page.
The cashback automatically gets deactivated after the end date, or you can explicitly deactivate it.
> The transaction will not be processed in the following cases:
>   * If the fee is more than 100 SATS as of now.
>   * If your account's available balance is not sufficient as per the cashback amount and the network fees .As soon as the ongoing cashback withdrawal request is processed successfully, the system is ready to process another request.
> 

#### [Cashback typesCopied!](https://docs.tryspeed.com/docs/send-payments/cashback#cashback-types)
  * You can create two types of cashback from the Speed web application. Let us see them in detail.
    * **Fixed Amount Cashback:** The reward amount is predefined and fixed by you. So, if your consumer makes a successful payment, a QR code for this fixed amount will be generated and shown to them to avail cashback.
    * **Percentage Amount Cashback:** The cashback amount is not predefined, but you can specify a percentage. A QR code with the cashback amount is generated after your customer completes the transaction. Based on the percentage you specified, this cashback amount is a percentage of the customer's payment.


> The cashback amount will always be less than your customer's payment.
#### [Cashback statusesCopied!](https://docs.tryspeed.com/docs/send-payments/cashback#cashback-statuses)
Throughout its lifetime, cashback transitions through two statuses, as mentioned below.
`active`
Upon creation, cashback remains active until the end date or is explicitly marked as deactivated by the user.
`deactivated`
After the end date, the cashback is automatically marked deactivated, or you can explicitly do the same.

#### [Use case it solvesCopied!](https://docs.tryspeed.com/docs/send-payments/cashback#use-case-it-solves)
You can associate a single cashback with multiple Speed receive-payment resources. Hence, you can create a cashback for any offer/scheme you want to give your customers, where you want to reward them with some funds once they pay. Some of those scenarios are mentioned below.
  * **Incentive for Spending:** Cashback programs encourage customers to make purchases by offering a financial reward. By providing a portion of the purchase price back to the customer, businesses aim to increase customer loyalty and stimulate spending.
  * **Customer Retention:** Cashback programs can help retain existing customers. By offering cashback on purchases, businesses provide added value to their customers, making them more likely to continue patronizing the same establishment or using the same payment method.
  * **Marketing and Promotion:** Cashback programs can serve as a marketing tool to promote specific products or services. By offering higher cashback rates on selected items or during certain promotional periods, businesses can drive customer interest and increase sales for those particular offerings.
  * **Building Brand Loyalty:** Cashback programs can contribute to building brand loyalty by establishing a positive association with a particular business or payment provider. When customers consistently receive cashback rewards, they may develop a preference for using the associated payment method or shopping at the affiliated stores.


> If the ongoing cashback withdrawal fails due to any reason, you need to worry as the withdrawal will be reversed, and the cashback amount deducted earlier will be restored to the account balance along with the network fees.
> **Note:** The transaction may not be processed in the following cases:
>   * If the fee is more than 100 SATS as of now.
>   * If your account's available balance is not sufficient as per the cashback amount and the network fees .As soon as the ongoing cashback withdrawal request is processed successfully, the system is ready to process another request.
>
